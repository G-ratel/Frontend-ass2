Spaghetti bolognaise

This is my first favorite recipe.

Ingredients:

  • mince beef
  • bacon chopped small
  • onion finely chopped
  • tomato puree
  • dried basil
  • tinned chopped tomato
  • garlic crushed

Directions:

  1. Fry the mince in a frying pan in small batches until it has coloured all over. Transfer to a large saucepan with a slotted spoon. If there is an excess of fat, mop it up with a piece of kitchen roll.
  2. Add the onion and bacon to the frying pan and cook for five minutes. Transfer into the pan with the mince.
  3. Add the tomato puree, tinned tomatoes, basil and garlic. Stir well and bring to a simmer, cover and cook for 60 minutes. Check a couple of times during cooking to see if the sauce is a bit thick. If it is just add some water to loosen it up.
  4. Serve with pasta, veggies and sprinkle some grated Parmesan cheese on the top.

Red velvet cake

This is my second favorite recipe.

Ingredients:

  • all-purpose flour
  • cornstarch
  • unsweetened cocoa powder
  • baking doda
  • salt
  • granulated sugar
  • unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
  • large eggs
  • vanilla extract
  • vegetable or canola oil
  • red food colouring
  • buttermilk
  • distilled wine vinegar

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F/180°C. Grease two 8-inch or 9-inch cake pans that are at least 2-inches high, and line the bottom of the pan with a round piece of parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, sift together flour, cornstarch, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a standing mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat together butter and sugar on medium speed until light and fluffy, 3-4 minutes. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl as necessary. Beat in oil. The batter might look a bit curdled, that's ok. Add eggs, one at a time, mixing well between each addition. Add vanilla and vinegar and beat until combined. Add red food coloring, until desired color is reached. With the mixer on low speed, add half of the dry ingredients and beat just until combined. Add buttermilk, followed by the rest of the dry ingredients. Do not over mix.
  4. Divide batter evenly between pans. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Allow cakes to cool in the pan on a wire rack until they're easy to handle, then remove from pan and allow to cool completely on a wire rack.

Chocolate cream pie

This is my third favorite recipe.

Ingredients:

  • digestive biscuits
  • butter
  • milk
  • sugar
  • corn flour
  • plain flour
  • eggs
  • double cream
  • dark chocolate
  • icing sugar for dusting

Directions:

  1. Place the digestive biscuits in a large bowl, and using the end of a rolling pin crush them until they resemble fine crumbs.
  2. Melt the butter gently in a small pan. When melted add the crushed biscuit and stir well.
  3. Press the biscuit and butter mix into the base and sides of a 8.5inch fluted flan tin. Chill in the fridge for 1.5hours before filling.
  4. For the filling, heat the milk gently in a saucepan.
  5. Meanwhile mix the sugar, flour, cornflour and eggs in a bowl. Stir in the heated milk, mix then return it all to the pan.
  6. Cook over a low heat until it thickens and just comes to boil. Stir continuously.
  7. Remove from the heat and stir in the butter and chocolate. Stir until the chocolate has melted and the sauce is smooth.
  8. Leave to cool slightly then pour the chocolate custard into the biscuit base. Level it off then dust with icing sugar, this prevents a skin forming as it cools.
  9. Put it back in the fridge to chill, about half an hour.
  10. When the pie is cold whip the cream and milk together until it is stiff enough to hold its shape. Spoon over the filling and grate some chocolate on the top.
  11. Enjoy.

Chicken Pie

This is my fourth favorite recipe.

Ingredients:

  • plain flour
  • margarine or butter
  • potatoes
  • milk
  • cooked chicken
  • frozen peas defrosted
  • cheese grated

Directions:

  1. Peel the potatoes and chop into chunks.
  2. Boil for 10-12 minutes until soft. Drain and leave in the pan without the lid on. This will allow any excess water to evaporate.
  3. Meanwhile place the margaine, flour and milk in a pan.
  4. Gently heat the pan, when the margarine has melted stir the sauce, keep stirring until it thickens.
  5. Let the sauce boil for 2 minutes, while continuing to stir. This boiling process, cooks the flour and avoids the sauce tasting of the flour.
  6. Mix the chicken and peas into the sauce.
  7. Mash the potatoes.
  8. Pour the sauce into a casserole dish and top with the mashed potato.

Oven Baked Risotto

This is my fifth favorite recipe.

Ingredients:

  • smoked bacon cut into small pieces
  • small chopped onion
  • butter
  • risotto rice
  • chicken chopped into bite sized pieces
  • chicken stock
  • tomato puree
  • parmesan cheese grated
  • cherry tomatoes

Directions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180’C fan/200’C no fan/Gas 6
  2. Fry the bacon and chicken in an ovenproof pan or casserole dish until the chicken no longer pink on the outside. This will take 5-8 minutes.
  3. Add the onion and butter. Cook for a further 4 minutes until the onion is soft.
  4. Add the rice and stir well to ensure all the grains of rice are coated.
  5. Pour in the stock and add the tomato puree and cherry tomatoes, give a quick stir then cover with a well fitting lid and bake in the oven for 15 minutes.
  6. Take the pan out of the oven, give the risotto a stir and taste the rice to see if it is cooked. If it needs more cooking about another 3 minutes will do. Add a little more hot water if it is a bit dry.
  7. Stir in about 5 grams of parmesan.
  8. Serve with parmesan sprinkled on the top and veggies.
